General Aung San

Founder of the Myanmar Armed Forces, and the Father of the Nation of modern-day Myanmar

1915 Born in Natmauk, Magway Region, Myanamr
1936 Became secretary of the students' union at Rangoon University and, with U Nu, led the students' strike there
1937-1938 Worked for the nationalist Dobama Asiayone("We-Burma Association")
1939 Became secretary-general of Dobama Asiayone
1940 He was contacted in China by the Japanese and they assisted him in raising a Burmese military force to aid them in their 1942 invasion of Burma, known as the "Burma Independence Army"
1945 Became skeptical of Japanese promises of Burmese independence, even if an unlikely Japanese victory were to occur, and was displeased with their treatment of Burmese forces.So, he switched his Burma National Army to the Allied cause.
Late 1946 Having helped form the Anti-Fascist People’s Freedom League (AFPFL), an underground movement of nationalists, in 1944, Aung San used that united front to become deputy chairman of Burma’s Executive Council
Jan 1947 After conferring with the British prime minister Clement Attlee in London, he announced an agreement that provided for Burma’s independence within one year.
April 1947 In the election for a constitutional assembly, his AFPFL won 196 of 202 seats. Though communists had denounced him as a “tool of British imperialism,” he supported a resolution for Burmese independence outside the British Commonwealth.
July 1947 The prime minister and six colleagues, including his brother, were assassinated in the council chamber in Rangoon while the executive council was in session.


"We want real freedom and not tinsel and sham of it. When we say real freedom, we meant the freedom not only to plan our political destiny but also the economy and defence of our country by ourselves."

(Address delivered to the Second Session of the Supreme Council of AFPFL, 16th May, 1946)
